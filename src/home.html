Why print(Name)?

print(Name) was chosen as our team’s name as we wanted to embody the ever-popular Python programming language. As an open-source, high-level programming language with extreme versatility in the fields of Data Mining, Data Science, AI (Artificial Intelligence), Machine learning, Web Development, Embedded System, Gaming, Testing, Automation (Desmond & McKenzie 2021), and so much more, we believe Python represents the diverse background of our team members. For instance, Thu originally came from the School of Business Management, and Vu from the School of Communication & Design. Nevertheless, each member’s skill set complements one another and we all come together to work towards a common goal - that is to ace this assignment and (hopefully) turn our project into a reality.

Group Process

Our group was not formed until the fourth week. In the beginning there was only Thu and My, then came Vu, and eventually Tri joined the group. Despite our late introduction to each other, and the fact that COVID-19 prevented any offline meeting until week 6, we were able to maintain a cooperative and supportive atmosphere within the team. Throughout our second assignment, My has become our de facto team leader, thanks largely to her dedication to the coursework and her sociable nature which helps to bond the group together. For our third assignment, she had gone out of her way to create a detailed task list to help everyone stay on track on what we needed to do, recorded weekly meeting minutes, and set up weekly calls to review our progress. That is not to say we did not encounter any hiccups. During the development of our demo, we ran into some technical issues which forced us to rethink on how our demo should be like. Nevertheless, we marched on, and thanks to Thu and Tri’s quick thinking, and coding skills, we were able to whip up an alternative prototype just in time for our presentation.
