Overview

Topic

In this project, we are creating a career exploration game that helps Vietnamese high school students to be better informed on which career path they should choose when entering university. As such, the game will feature a variety of roles in a wide range of industries e.g. IT, communication, finance, business, etc.

Motivation

For many Vietnamese high school students who are about to enter university, choosing a career pathway is often seen as a gamble simply because they lack the necessary knowledge and real-world resources to make an informed decision...

Landscape

Among countless career exploration websites/platforms, two stood out the most and they are Claim Your Future and Roadtrip Nation...

Detailed Description

Aims

The initial purpose of developing this game is to give the younger generation an overview of different working fields and a general day for a specific job. Even though there are many sources about high-paying jobs or the best careers in demand for the future, some youngsters still struggle when registering for the college entrance exam...

Plans and Progress

This assignment 3 is a complex project with multiple components that must be finished within a certain time range. As a result, we must develop plans from the start, monitor their implementation, and constantly evaluate their success. To begin, because assignment 3 is a continuation of assignment 2, the team ensured that they had a good grasp on the project concept prior to beginning work on it...

Scopes and Limits

Due to the time limits, our group has to redefine the scope and reduce the number of characters, interactions, industries, jobs, and tasks to create the finished product on time. Instead of making high-quality 3D games, we will stick to the basics of 2D games with simple but attractive designs and interactions...

Tools and Technologies

There are various tools and technologies that are required for the creation of the game as well as its continued development...

Testing

To begin testing, we must ensure that our code is functional and behaves appropriately. To do this, we will play the game and debug any issues that arise; after the technical portion is complete, we will move on to the social portion. We envision that our project will result in a game that inspires and guides people to pursue careers that are a good fit for them, and because this game requires a great deal of information to be completed, we recognize that we will need to solicit feedback from a large number of people in order to have test results for each personality type. Our method of testing the project is to promote it on social media, at our university, in class, and to urge people to give it a try. After accumulating sufficient data, we will attempt to develop our game and round out the final product.

Timeframe and Risk

This is the first time our team attended an IT course in RMIT school, so everything is new to us, we never experienced coding before nor making a game. We have been learning to do anything from scratch, but there are a limited number of things we can learn in a short period of time and after 3 months of studying in this course we cannot say that the project outcome of assignment 3 is perfect but we surely can say that we are proud of it...

Group processes and communications

As a result of our team's bonding since the start of assignment 2, we have gained sufficient knowledge of one another's strengths and weaknesses to more efficiently distribute work and ensure that everyone is doing what they do best, while also avoiding communication breakdowns. Due to the project's size, we have divided the work among ourselves and then meet in person once a week to ensure that our workload is manageable and that we are progressing as quickly as possible through the use of our collective knowledge. Our group also meets every Saturday night at 8 p.m in which we communicate via messenger's call to discuss what we did over the weekend and what we will do next, as well as any issues that may arise while working on the assignment. In the meeting, we use google docs to write down what we have discussed and what we have done. So that we can later review it or for any member who cannot attend the meeting can read it and know what we have discussed and know what to do next.
